About

Research Focus

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at KAUST, working under the supervision of Professor Mohamed Elhoseiny. My research focuses on advancing multimodal learning through Vision-Language Models (VLMs), with applications spanning 2D/3D visual perception and generation.

Thesis Topic ‚Äî Unsolicited Advice in the Era of Large Models. My research investigates how new capabilities can be incorporated into large-scale models in an efficient and principled manner, beyond the dominant paradigm of scaling model size and data. I argue that certain forms of knowledge‚Äîparticularly physical understanding and fine-grained visual grounding‚Äîremain challenging for large models to acquire implicitly through scale alone. Accordingly, my work explores lightweight, targeted training interventions that introduce appropriate inductive biases without architectural expansion.

Currently, I am interning at Adobe London, where I work on extending multimodal generation models to enable precise grounding and interleaved reasoning alongside advanced editing capabilities. My work bridges the gap between perception and generation in modern AI systems.

Prior to KAUST, I spent nearly four years at Valeo as a Senior Machine Learning Researcher, where I tackled real-world challenges in autonomous driving, including extreme hardware limitations and industrial-scale model deployment. I was recognized as a Top Innovator and Top Researcher for 2021-2022 across Valeo's Egypt site.
